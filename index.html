Murray Sawchuck

  • Character Played:Murray The Magician
  • Location: Tropicana - Laugh Factory
  • Years in Show: 4th year at the Tropicana (Did 3 years starting in 2012 then bumped to Planet Hollywood then came back in 2018 to Tropicana)
  • Hometown: Burnaby, British Columbia, Canada (Near Vancouver)

Background/Training/Education: Started magic when I was 7 years old. Toured the world for 18 years performing in Belgium, France, Korea, Tahiti, Lebanon, England, Germany, China, Japan and many other countries. I’m a performer, lecture, writer, actor and comedian. I also have a degree in Broadcast communications and a minor in marketing.

Passions outside of work: Working on my old cars; a '73 Corvette Stingray, '69 Pontiac Grand Prix and 1991 Nissan Hardbody Pickup. I also have 3 rescue chihuahuas, I’ve rescued 5 chihuahuas in total. It was a "look mom and dad I made it!" moment. Someone captured that exact time when I walked into the audience to give them both a hug and a kiss, and that photo hangs on my wall in my house today. It means even more to me now, after losing my Dad 4 years ago. Mom and dad were married 54 years!

Jaime DeRocker

  • Show: Nevada Ballet Theatre
  • Years working with NBT: I am about to start my third season.
  • Hometown: Oneida, New York

Favorite Roles I’ve danced with Nevada Ballet Theatre: Gerald Arpino’s Light Rain Pas de deux with Steven Goforth, George Balanchine’s Serenade, Yuri Possokhov’s Firebird, Ben Stevenson’s Dracula, Paul Taylor’s Company B, to name a few of my favorites.

Background/training education: I grew up dancing and training in both a competition dance school and an R.A.D. accredited ballet school at the same time. At 15 years old, I decided to finish my high school training at The Harid Conservatory. From there I had the wonderful opportunity to gain experience and dance with the Colorado Ballet, Ballet West and Festival Ballet Providence. I attended a summer intensive in 2015 at Nederlands Dans Theatre that changed my life and perspective on dance.

Passions outside of work: I absolutely love music, going to live shows, supporting my fellow performer/entertainer friends in other shows around Vegas, and exploring all the new and exciting things that Vegas has to offer.

Favorite thing to do in Vegas: My favorite thing to do in Vegas, hands down, is take advantage of the never-ending offerings of live shows. I never turn down an invitation to attend a show. I think I’ve seen The Beatles LOVE almost 10 times!

Most memorable/satisfying moment in the show: I will never forget the curtain going up at The Smith Center (my favorite theatre I have performed in to date, and I’m so incredibly lucky to be able to perform there) at the opening of George Balanchine’s Serenade. The company last performed it in the fall of 2017. The opening image that is revealed includes the entire female corps de ballet. It is so stunning, and the audience immediately began to applaud. In that moment, a tear streamed down my face and I could not have been happier. I was so proud and excited. I knew I was exactly where I needed to be.

Most memorable/satisfying moment in the show: We are happy that the show is doing so well, and audiences from all over the world enjoy what we do. We are thrilled to have performed over 850 successful shows so far and to have won four "Best of Vegas” awards in four different categories.

Mike Vargovich

  • Show: Clown Bar
  • Location: Majestic Repertory Theatre
  • Character played: Dusty, aka The Crooner
  • Hometown: Buffalo, NY

Years in show: One year. The original Las Vegas Clown Bar cast was put together two years ago and I’ve been involved since August 2018.

Background training/education: Zero formal clown training. I just put on the face and let it take over. I learned from watching everyone in the original cast transform from people I knew into their respective clowns. I’m very much an observer. I never took well to classes or even workshops in the past. I’m someone who just kind of needs to go out and do it.

Passions outside of work: I have an insatiable thirst for reading biographies on comedians, and would call myself a novice book collector. When I joined Clown Bar I did a deep dive on Emmett Kelly and “Weary Willie,” to whom my Dusty is obviously a direct nod, and that led to an appreciation of clowning as an art, and of its place in history. Then I feel like I’m going on stage with some context. Favorite thing to do in Vegas: Anything that puts me in touch with the history of this place. I’ve served as impromptu tour guide for tourists waiting to cross the street on the Strip just because the history of Las Vegas fascinates me so much. I’ll spend all day in the Mob Museum or exploring a barren ghost town if you leave me to it.

Most memorable/satisfying moment in the show: Just being able to perform at Majestic Rep. There’s an energy in that space you don’t feel anywhere else. As an independently run storefront theatre it encompasses the best of what that distinction implies. It’s gritty, it’s grassroots, and when you’re there you feel like your witness to the beginning of a larger vision. Troy Heard challenges his actors as well as his audiences in the tradition of so many groundbreaking theatre companies.

Jordan Kai Burnett

  • Show: MAGIC MIKE LIVE Las Vegas
  • Location: Hard Rock Hotel
  • Character: Emcee
  • Years in show: Less than a year
  • Hometown: Astoria, Queens, New York.

Background training/education: I have a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Musical Theater from Emerson College, and also studied at the University of California, Berkeley, The Groundlings and Circle in the Square. I have had leading roles in “Scissorhands” the musical and “Romy & Michele's HS Reunion the Musical.” Lastly, I am the Co-creator of "Daisy and Jordan's Sunday Brunch of Shame” with Tony Award-winner Daisy Eagan.

Passions outside of work: Outside of work, my biggest passions are my band, @STOPTHRUST, and my dog, Ernie. When I'm not at MAGIC MIKE LIVE or taking my pup on an adventure, I am somewhere in Las Vegas working on "band stuff." Sounds pretty rock 'n roll when I say it like that... but I love writing and playing music with these boys more than I can say. Can't wait to bring them out to Vegas and show Sin City how we do it.

Favorite thing to do in Vegas: Since I'm still fairly new to town, my favorite thing to do in Vegas is search for the best dive bar and the best pizza. So far, Double Down Saloon has my heart, but I’m still in search for that pizza that will hit the spot when I'm feeling homesick. Other than that, Market in the Alley in downtown is incredible, especially Excuse Moi, which is a speakeasy with a silent disco inside. It’s heaven.

Most memorable/satisfying moment in the show: There are two moments in the show that gets me every night. The first is in the opening number; I'm surrounded by the guys right before the beat of the next song drops and it feels like we are gearing up to go into battle. For a moment, it is about us and the show, and I feel powerful as hell. The second is at the end of the show, before my final exit, when I get to have a sincere, albeit cheeky, moment with my fellow queens in the audience. Even though we are having a laugh, I get to remind them that who they are, just as they are, is enough. Again, in empowering other women, I get to feel powerful too.
